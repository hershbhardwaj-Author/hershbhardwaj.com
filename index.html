I grew up in India studying science. Not because I chose to — because that was the path my family needed me to take. At 22, something broke open. I walked into a literature class, and six months later I was on a plane to the UK — the first person in my entire generation to leave for education abroad.

I arrived at the University of Liverpool with a Masters in English Literature already behind me, and came out with a postgraduate qualification in Literary Studies and Film. I arrived without contacts, without a network, without anyone waiting for me. What I had was a page of an idea.

That single page found its way to the world's publishing establishment. Four of the world's top publishers wanted the book. I hadn't pitched anyone. The work found them.

I went on to start an independent press — Shreem Books — with zero capital. Then Covid came. I lost everything. Not just the business — everything. I made myself one promise in those days: stay alive one more day. Just one more. For the day when it would be worth it.

I rebuilt. And at 46, I am building Author's Forge — a 20-week publishing incubator for serious experts who have something real to say and deserve a path to say it that doesn't require anyone's permission.

This is not a pivot. This is the whole story, finally making sense.

The Book

A to B — From Adversity
to Breakthrough

✦ HarperCollins Business India

Every great brand gets one night with the king. Win it, and you earn another. Lose it — and you're gone by morning.

A to B draws on the ancient wisdom of Scheherazade — the princess who survived 1,001 nights not through strength, but through storytelling — and applies it to the way the greatest brands in the world build loyalty, survive adversity, and keep their audience coming back. Apple. Google. PlayStation. Every one of them is Scheherazade. The story must never end.

A roadmap for anyone who needs to move from adversity to breakthrough — with the most powerful tool available: their own story.
